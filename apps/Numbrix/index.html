Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How do I play Numbrix!?

Fill in the missing numbers on a 9√ó9 grid so that every number from 1 to 81 forms a continuous path. Numbers connect horizontally or vertically ‚Äî no diagonal connections allowed. Some numbers are already placed as clues. Use logic to figure out where each missing number belongs. Every puzzle has exactly one solution.

2. What are the difficulty levels?

Numbrix! offers three difficulty levels based on how many clues are provided:

  • Easy: 35‚Äì40 clues ‚Äî Relaxing, great for beginners

  • Hard: 18‚Äì24 clues ‚Äî Expert level, for seasoned puzzlers

3. How does the chapter system work?

The game has 10 chapters with 20 levels each (200 levels total). Difficulty increases progressively as you advance:

  • Chapter 1: Easy
  • Chapter 2: Easy ‚Üí Medium (transition)
  • Chapters 3‚Äì4: Medium
  • Chapter 5: Medium ‚Üí Hard (transition)

4. How do daily challenges work?

Every day you get three fresh puzzles ‚Äî one Easy, one Medium, and one Hard. Complete at least one to maintain your daily streak. Complete all three for a "Perfect Day." Each daily puzzle rewards 15 coins (45 total per day).

5. How does the coin system work?

  • Level completion: Earn 10 coins (15 for 3-star completion)
  • Chapter completion: Earn 50 bonus coins
  • Daily challenges: Earn 15 coins per puzzle
  • Watch ads: Earn 50 coins (150 for Premium users)
  • Hints: Cost 50 coins each ‚Äî reveals one correct number

6. How does the star rating work?

Each puzzle is rated 1 to 3 stars based on completion time and hint usage:

  • 3 Stars: No hints used and fast completion time
  • 2 Stars: At most 1 hint and moderate time
  • 1 Star: Completed the puzzle (any time or hints)

7. What achievements can I unlock?

Numbrix! features 18 achievements across five categories:

  • Streak: 3-day, 7-day, 14-day, 30-day, 60-day, 100-day, and 365-day streaks
  • Completion: Complete 10, 50, 100, or 200 levels
  • Speed: Beat Easy under 1 min, Medium under 3 min, Hard under 5 min
  • Perfect: 10 consecutive 3-star levels, or 20 levels without hints
  • Daily: 7 or 30 consecutive perfect days
